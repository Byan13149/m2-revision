Training Best Practices

Lectures: L07–L08

Karpathy's Training Recipe

Neural networks fail silently

They can fit around bugs. They learn "something" even when your pipeline is broken. Be a conspiracy theorist: don't believe anything is working; verify every step.

  1. Become one with the data. Spend hours inspecting your dataset before touching any ML. Look for duplicates, corrupted labels, class imbalances, strange min/max values.
  2. Build a simple end-to-end pipeline. Start with a linear model. Confirm the loss starts at the expected value (e.g., $-\log(1/K)$ for $K$-class classification). Beat an input-independent baseline.
  3. Overfit on a small batch (~1%). Use a slightly larger model. Drive training loss to near zero. This confirms no hidden bugs and that all "wires connect."
  4. Increase model capacity. Try to overfit the full training set. If you can't, something is wrong.
  5. Regularize. Gradually add weight decay, dropout, data augmentation, etc. Monitor validation loss.
  6. Tune hyperparameters at small scale first. Spot trends before committing to expensive large-scale runs.

Tuning Methods

  1. Manual: Requires understanding of algorithm. Most efficient for experts.
  2. Grid search: Simple, exhaustive. Expensive with many hyperparameters.
  3. Random search: Often better than grid (explores more of each dimension independently).
  4. Coarse-to-fine: Random search, then zoom into promising regions. Most used in practice.
  5. Bayesian optimization: Maintains a probabilistic model of performance. Most efficient but harder to set up.

Experiment Tracking

Options: TensorBoard (baseline), Weights & Biases (most popular in research), Sacred, Neptune.
