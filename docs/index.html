M2: Deep Learning — Exam Revision Guide

Course: MPhil in Data Intensive Science, Cambridge — Lent Term

Lecturer: Miles Cranmer • Textbook: Understanding Deep Learning by Simon J.D. Prince

Assessment: 50% final coursework, 50% exam. Exam questions are similar to those in the textbook.

Exam Scope

Lectures 1–21 are examinable. Lecture 22 (Research Methods) is not examinable. PGMs (L16) and some RL material are not covered in Prince; use the uploaded lecture notes for those.

Topics Overview

Neural Networks Review

MLPs, activation functions, universal approximation, deep networks, composing piecewise-linear functions.

L01–L03 • Prince Ch 3–5

Loss Functions & Training

Maximum likelihood, cross-entropy, backpropagation, SGD, Adam, momentum, learning rate schedules.

L02–L03 • Prince Ch 5–7

Regularization & Generalization

Bias-variance tradeoff, double descent, L2/L1 regularization, dropout, early stopping, data augmentation.

L03 • Prince Ch 8–9

Convolutional Neural Networks

Convolution, padding, stride, channels, pooling, equivariance, AlexNet, VGG, YOLO, U-Net.

L04–L05 • Prince Ch 10–11

PyTorch Core

Tensors, autograd, nn.Module, training loops, DataLoader, MLP vs CNN comparison.

L06

Best Practices

Karpathy's recipe, debugging, loss curve diagnostics, hyperparameter tuning strategies.

L07–L08

Transformers

Self-attention, Q/K/V, multi-head attention, positional encoding, encoder/decoder, BERT, GPT, ViT.

L08–L10 • Prince Ch 12

LLMs & Decoding

Decoding strategies, temperature, top-k, beam search, RAG, multimodal LLMs, CLIP, speculative decoding.

L11

RL Foundations

MDPs, policies, value functions, Bellman equations, exploration vs exploitation.

L12–L13 • Prince Ch 19

RL Algorithms

Dynamic programming, Monte Carlo, TD learning, Q-learning, SARSA, DQN, policy gradients, REINFORCE.

L14–L15 • Prince Ch 19

Probabilistic Graphical Models

Directed PGMs, factorised joints, plates, Bayes rule, MLE vs MAP, generative stories.

L16 • Lecture notes

Variational Autoencoders

Latent variable models, ELBO, KL divergence, reparameterization trick, encoder-decoder.

L17 • Prince Ch 17

Normalizing Flows

Change of variables, Jacobian determinants, coupling layers, autoregressive flows, exact likelihoods.

L18 • Prince Ch 16

Diffusion Models

DDPM, forward/reverse process, noise schedules, ELBO, denoising score matching, classifier-free guidance.

L19–L21 • Prince Ch 18

Study Strategy

The prof says exam questions are similar to textbook problems. Work through the exercises at the end of each Prince chapter for the best preparation. Focus on being able to derive key results (ELBO, Bellman equations, backprop, change-of-variables) and explain architectural choices (why residual connections, why multi-head attention, why reparameterization trick).
